Sentimental Analysis of College Reviews using Machine Learning Techniques

An extensive exploration of sentiment analysis applied to a collection of reviews gathered through a Google Form circulated to the college student. Through various data preprocessing techniques, and the application of various machine learning models, we aimed to uncover insights into the sentiments expressed in the reviews. Initially, dataset was analyzed, this analysis revealed the distribution of sentiments, enabling us to gain insights into the general sentiment tendencies of the students. To prepare the textual data for training, we performed extensive preprocessing techniques, including text cleaning, tokenization, stop word removal, and lemmatization/stemming. TF-IDF and count vectorizer are used to transform text data into a numerical representation. Our study included the implementation of a range of machine learning models, including Support Vector Machine (SVM), K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Random Forest, Adaptive Boosting, and Gradient Boosting. All machine learning model was trained, validated, and evaluated using suitable metrics. These evaluations allowed us to measure the models' efficiency in sentiment classification. The research contributes to the field of sentiment analysis by demonstrating the effectiveness of various machine learning models in classifying sentiments within a specific community, as represented by the reviews collected through a Google Form in our college.
